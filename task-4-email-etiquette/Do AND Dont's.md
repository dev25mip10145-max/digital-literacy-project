#Social Media Checklist for College Students

The Do's: Building a Positive Digital Footprint
1.Do showcase your projects: Use platforms to share your academic milestones, coding repositories, or creative content like YouTube videos.
2.Do review privacy settings: Regularly check who can see your posts and restrict personal updates to close friends and family.
3.Do network professionally: Actively connect with peers, professors, and industry professionals on platforms like LinkedIn.
4.Do think before you post: Assume that anything you share online, even privately, can become public and permanent.
5.Do engage positively: Share insightful articles, offer constructive feedback, and support your peers' achievements.

The Don'ts: Avoiding Digital Pitfalls
1.Don't use unprofessional language: Avoid profanity, offensive jokes, or aggressive tone, as recruiters and academic staff often review social profiles.
2.Don't overshare personal information: Never publicly post your phone number, exact real-time location, or sensitive documents like your student ID.
3.Don't engage in online arguments: Avoid getting drawn into heated, public debates or flame wars in comment sections.
4.Don't plagiarize content: Always give proper credit when sharing someone else's research, artwork, or code.
5.Don't ignore your digital footprint: Don't assume old, embarrassing posts won't be found; periodically audit and clean up your past content.
