For this segment of the project, I utilized **Google Forms** to design a targeted "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" for my first-year peers. I practically applied the core principles of digital citizenship by structuring an interactive assessment that tests fundamental concepts such as two-factor authentication, identifying phishing attempts, and maintaining professional digital communication. Building this form allowed me to practice instructional design and logical data collection structuring, ensuring the questions were both engaging and functionally sound.

Academically, mastering streamlined data collection tools is incredibly valuable. Creating structured forms will be essential for gathering primary data in future research methodologies or organizing collaborative peer study sessions for complex subjects like discrete mathematics and object-oriented programming. Furthermore, internalizing these digital literacy concepts directly supports my broader efforts to communicate scientific topics securely and effectively on my YouTube channels. By ensuring my digital practices are rigorous and professional, I can safely manage collaborative code repositories for C++ projects, protect my online accounts, and build a credible, easily verifiable academic presence throughout my degree.
