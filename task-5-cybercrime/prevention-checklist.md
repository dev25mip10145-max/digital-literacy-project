#Stay Safe Online: A Cyber-Defense Checklist for College Students

Financial & UPI Safety
1.Never enter your PIN to receive money:** A UPI PIN is only required to *send* money or check your balance, never to receive funds.
2.Verify before you pay:** Always double-check the receiver's name and Virtual Payment Address (VPA) before scanning QR codes at campus canteens or local shops.
3.Beware of fake internship fees:** Legitimate companies will never ask you to pay a "registration fee" or "equipment deposit" via UPI for a summer internship or part-time job.

Account & Device Security
4.Lock down your accounts with 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all critical accounts, especially your primary email, GitHub, and social media.
5.Dodge the public Wi-Fi trap: Avoid logging into university portals, bank accounts, or making transactions while connected to unsecured public networks in cafes or libraries.
6.Guard your OTPs: Never share an One-Time Password (OTP) with anyone over the phone, even if they claim to be from the bank, tech support, or university administration.
7.Use unique passwords: Do not recycle the same password across multiple platforms. If one site suffers a data breach, all your accounts become vulnerable.
8.Think before you click  Treat urgent emails or SMS messages containing links with extreme caution, especially those claiming your account is blocked or demanding immediate action.
