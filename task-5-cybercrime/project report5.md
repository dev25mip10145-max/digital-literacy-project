#Research Reflection & Habit Change

While researching digital safety and cybercrime for this project, the most surprising realization was how heavily modern scams rely on psychological manipulation rather than advanced technical hacking. I always assumed cybercriminals used complex code to breach accounts, but the reality is that phishing and social engineering—like fake KYC updates or urgent messages mimicking official administration—are far more common and effective. It was alarming to see how easily someone can be tricked into handing over their secure UPI PIN just out of panic or a false sense of urgency. As a direct result of this research, the one habit I am permanently changing is my approach to public Wi-Fi. I will no longer log into sensitive platforms or make financial transactions while connected to unsecured networks around campus, ensuring my digital presence remains secure.
